# Hack The Box

Hack The Box is one of the primary platforms I use to practice offensive security in a legal, controlled environment. It offers intentionally vulnerable machines and challenges designed to simulate real-world attack scenarios.

## Why this directory exists

Learning cybersecurity without hands-on practice is theory without grounding. HTB forces me to actually apply what I read about — enumeration, exploitation, privilege escalation — in environments that behave like real systems. The goal is not to collect flags but to understand the decision-making and methodology behind each step.

## What the need is

I need a structured place to document what I did, why I did it, and what I learned. Raw tool output is useless without reflection. These write-ups exist to capture my thought process, including dead ends and mistakes, not just the clean path to a flag.

## What to expect

Each subdirectory contains write-ups organized by type:

- `machines/` — full machine compromises, from initial enumeration to root
- `challenges/` — standalone challenges such as forensics, crypto, or web tasks

Each file follows a loose structure: initial approach, enumeration output, decision-making, exploitation, and afterthoughts. Sensitive data such as flags and credentials are redacted.
