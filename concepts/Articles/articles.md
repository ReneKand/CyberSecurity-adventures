# Articles

This directory contains analyses of real-world security incidents, notable CVEs, and publicly documented breaches across web, software, and infrastructure security.

## Why this directory exists

Reading about vulnerabilities in the abstract is one thing. Seeing how they played out in production systems — with real consequences — is another. Articles like the XZ Utils backdoor or major supply chain attacks show that security failures are rarely just technical. They involve trust, timing, social engineering, and oversight gaps that are hard to learn from theory alone.

## What the need is

Incident analysis builds pattern recognition. The more real cases I work through, the better I get at identifying the conditions that precede a breach — not just the exploit itself but the chain of decisions and oversights that made it possible. That kind of thinking does not come from labs alone.

## What to expect

Each file covers a single incident, CVE, or case study. Entries include:

- Background on the affected system or software
- What happened and how the vulnerability or attack worked technically
- Why it was possible — the conditions, assumptions, or oversights involved
- What was done in response and what could have prevented it
- Personal takeaways and observations

The goal is understanding, not just summarizing. These are not news articles — they are dissections.
